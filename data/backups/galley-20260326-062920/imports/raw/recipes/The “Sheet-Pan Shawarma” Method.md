
The “Sheet-Pan Shawarma” Method

This technique recreates the high heat + fat dripping effect of a shawarma spit.

Ingredients

(for about 600–700 g chicken)

600–700 g chicken thigh, thinly sliced

2 tbsp olive oil

120 g Greek yogurt

4 garlic cloves, grated

2 tbsp lemon juice


Spices

2 tsp cumin

2 tsp coriander

1½ tsp paprika

1 tsp turmeric

1 tsp cinnamon

1 tsp chili flakes

½ tsp cardamom

1 tsp salt

½ tsp black pepper



---

Step 1 — Marinate

Mix everything together and coat the chicken well.

Marinate:

minimum: 1 hour

best: overnight



---

Step 2 — Spread the Chicken Correctly

Preheat oven to 250 °C.

Line a baking tray with parchment.

Important trick:

Spread the chicken in a thin single layer so pieces slightly overlap but do not pile up.

This helps create browned edges like shawarma slices.


---

Step 3 — Roast at Very High Heat

Cook:

12–15 minutes

The chicken should:

caramelize

get slightly charred edges

stay juicy



---

Step 4 — The Shawarma Finish (Critical Step)

Remove the tray and:

1. Chop the chicken roughly.


2. Toss it with the juices on the tray.


3. Return to oven 5 minutes more.



This creates the crispy shawarma bits you normally only get from a spit.


---

Step 5 — Pan Finish (Optional but Amazing)

Heat a skillet with 1 tbsp olive oil.

Fry the roasted chicken 1–2 minutes.

This adds:

more browning

crispy edges

deeper flavor



---

Classic Shawarma Wrap Assembly

Spread on flatbread:

garlic sauce (toum or yogurt sauce)

lettuce

tomato

pickles

chicken

pickled onion

sumac


Roll tightly.

Toast the wrap in a pan with:

olive oil

paprika

sumac


until crispy.


---

Pro Shawarma Tips

1. Always use chicken thigh

Restaurants almost never use breast.

2. Don’t overcrowd the pan

Steam kills browning.

3. Add acid at the end

A squeeze of lemon after cooking wakes up the spices.
