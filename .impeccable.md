## Design Context

### Users
Primary user is the owner of a self-hosted home recipe archive, currently used by one person and accessible across desktop, tablet, and phone on the home network. The core jobs are to capture recipes before they disappear, preserve provenance, refine them over time, retrieve them quickly, and cook from them under real kitchen conditions. The product should scale to a broader audience later without losing its archive-first domestic utility.

### Brand Personality
Premium, calm, operational.

The interface should communicate trust, ownership, privacy, persistence, and quiet confidence. It should feel like a trusted domestic command library rather than a social app, content feed, generic productivity SaaS, or AI-first novelty surface. Copy should stay clear, restrained, technically literate, and practical.

### Aesthetic Direction
Use a dark-only visual language built around a near-black shell, graphite working surfaces, charcoal panels, muted bone-grey text, and restrained green and amber signals. The recommended direction is the "Strongest" Sevastolink interpretation: dossier-like recipe pages, pronounced metadata strips, clear seams and rails, disciplined panel hierarchy, premium but restrained typography, and very limited accent use.

Reference direction is internal rather than tied to a third-party app: galley dossier + operational archive + premium dark utility.

Anti-references:
- terminal cosplay
- fake shell prompt UI
- cyberpunk glow
- gaming dashboard density
- decorative telemetry clutter
- content-feed or lifestyle recipe app styling
- overly sparse luxury minimalism with weak usability

Accessibility expectations are hard constraints: high contrast, readable body and metadata text, clear focus states, large tap targets, low cognitive load, and arm's-length kitchen readability. Motion should stay brief and purposeful, avoiding dramatic sweeps, jitter, decorative loops, or spectacle.

### Design Principles
- Structure over spectacle. Identity comes from hierarchy, seams, metadata, spacing, and retrieval clarity, not decoration.
- Archive first. Every screen should prioritize finding, understanding, using, improving, and preserving recipes.
- Kitchen readability is mandatory. Larger type, stronger spacing, reduced chrome, stable controls, and non-color-only progress cues take precedence over visual flourish.
- Premium through discipline. Use tonal layering, restrained typography, and limited accent color instead of loud effects or trend-driven styling.
- AI stays subordinate. AI can assist, but the archive remains the product; source visibility, reviewability, and user control are mandatory.
