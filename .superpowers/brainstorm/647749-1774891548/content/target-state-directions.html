Galley Target-State Directions

Reference-inspired options grounded in the archive-first docs. Click one if a direction feels right.

Option A

Archive Spine

Treat Galley primarily as a premium personal recipe archive with stronger intake, deeper recipe dossiers, and structured source trust.

Strengths

  • Best fit with current local-first and archive-first docs
  • Builds naturally from existing intake, recipe detail, and kitchen mode
  • Keeps the product calm and operational

Risks

  • Pantry and storage workflows stay secondary
  • Could feel too library-like and not proactive enough day to day

Reference pull: Tandoor, Mealie docs, Tandoor docs, StillTasty evidence style

Option B

Storage Intelligence

Center Galley on fridge, pantry, leftovers, and shelf-life guidance, with recipes becoming the execution layer after a storage decision.

Strengths

  • Distinctive versus generic recipe managers
  • Strong tie to StillTasty and Does It Go Bad patterns
  • Creates daily utility even when not actively cooking

Risks

  • Can pull focus away from recipe archival depth
  • Needs stronger data model and editorial safety rules

Reference pull: StillTasty, Does It Go Bad, MyFridgeFood

Option C

Operational Cooking Hub

Keep the archive as the system core, then add pantry/storage intelligence and meal-planning layers around it for a single-household operating system.

Strengths

  • Best match for a balanced mix of archive, pantry, and planning
  • Lets Galley improve on the reference sites instead of copying them
  • Supports phased rollout without breaking the current product identity

Risks

  • Needs clear prioritization to avoid product sprawl
  • Requires stronger shell, route, and component-system planning

Reference pull: Mealie, Tandoor, GrubPick signals, StillTasty, MyFridgeFood

Recommended product posture

Archive core -> Storage awareness -> Planning + action

One household. Source preserved. Trust stated. Shelf-life guidance stays advisory, never fake certainty. AI remains optional and review-centered.
